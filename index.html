Methodology

ContradictionMap systematically identifies contradictions between overlapping meta-analyses within and across Cochrane systematic reviews. The pipeline recomputes 5,279 meta-analyses from 501 reviews, identifies 881 overlapping pairs (sharing at least 2 primary studies), and classifies each pair into one of three contradiction types:

  • Direct contradiction — Effect estimates point in opposite directions (one harmful, one protective) and both are statistically significant.
  • Significance contradiction — Effects point in the same direction but one is statistically significant (p < 0.05) and the other is not.
  • Magnitude contradiction — Both significant and same direction, but effect sizes differ by more than 2-fold.

Overlap is measured by the Jaccard index of shared primary studies. Quality correlation uses audit flags from the MetaAudit pipeline (CRITICAL, FAIL, WARN severity levels). All analyses use a fixed random seed (42) for reproducibility.
